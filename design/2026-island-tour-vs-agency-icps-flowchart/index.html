Island Tour vs Agency ICPs — Architectural Comparison

Traveler & agency journey comparison across 6 ICPs, with shared vs different patterns made explicit.

Anchor ICP: Island Tour (single-destination tour specialist). Compared against: Travel Advisor (Fora) · Boutique/Luxury · Honeymoon · Safari · Multi-Day Guided Tour. Two perspectives: Traveler journey (top) · Agency journey (bottom). Companion to: the full report.

Shared across all ICPs Island Tour (anchor) Travel Advisor (Fora) Boutique/Luxury Honeymoon Safari Multi-Day Tour

Part 1 Traveler Journey — Browse → Discover → Compare → Book

Every ICP's traveler goes through land → browse → discover → compare → book → pay → travel → review. The differences are in how they discover (search vs curated), what they compare (one product vs many components), and how they book (instant vs quote vs scheduled-departure picker). The Island Tour path is highlighted with a heavier border; the other ICPs branch off the shared spine.

1 · Land

Google deep-local-intent, IG, advisor referral, Virtuoso, trade partner

2 · Browse

Region / destination / theme / trip-style pages

3 · Discover

Tour detail page; "Best of Maldives" collections; ~10–25 fixed products

4 · Compare

Variants, COMPARE tool, alternative camps/resorts, "see similar"

5 · Book

Instant book with date picker, full or deposit+balance

6 · Pay

Card / link / schedule; trust account if ATOL/ABTA

7 · Receive

Confirmation + manifest + client portal access

8 · Travel

Day-of contact, local guide, in-country support

9 · Review

Post-trip email → Trustpilot / TripAdvisor / public review

Where the other ICPs branch off the Island Tour path

Discover — Advisor: most discovery happens off-site (IG, blog, email). The Tripwheel-style "browse" surface is the advisor's curated portfolio, not a search-driven catalog. Compare = mental model, not a UI widget.
Book — Boutique/Luxury: NOT instant book. Clicking "Enquire" opens a quote form, then 4-step human flow (Scott Dunn: "Make an Enquiry → Speak to an Expert → Receive a Quote → Book"). Price is bespoke, finalized in the proposal.
Compare — Honeymoon: side-by-side resort COMPARE tool (Wedaways: 8 resorts, COMPARE button). The "compare" step IS the booking aid — they don't compare trips, they compare venues.
Book — Safari: "Enquire Now" (Wilderness Safaris) → per-camp holds → human confirms. Per-camp inventory ("6 tents on Aug 12") is the constraint, not the date.
Book — Multi-Day Tour: scheduled-departure picker with a code per departure (Intrepid: SSEP / SASY / SSKU). Date is primary; seats are the inventory unit ("5 spots left on Oct 14").
Pay — All non-Island-Tour ICPs: deposit+balance with hard final-payment cutoff. Boutique Luxury + Safari may sit in a regulated trust account (ATOL/ABTA) until travel completion.
Pay — Honeymoon: room-block deposit from couple, then individual guest payments against the room block. Multi-payer ledger per booking.
Review — Advisor: usually internal/private, not public. The advisor's reputation is on LinkedIn + referrals, not on the operator site.

Part 2 Agency Journey — Build → Manage → Publish

The agency side. How the operator/advisor builds their website, manages their inventory, takes inquiries, confirms bookings, and publishes content. The shared backbone is "tenancy + auth + CMS + CRM + booking + payment + media library + mobile app" — the universal SaaS core. What differs is the mode the workspace runs in (fixed-catalogue vs quote vs scheduled-departure vs per-date-inventory) and which modules are turned on (room-block, trade-portal, commission-split, schools-program, conservation-pages, concierge-247, trip-style-taxonomy).

1 · Build site

CMS + templates + custom domain + brand

2 · Add product

Trip / itinerary / hotel / activity record

3 · Manage inventory

~10–25 fixed tours; date + per-tour cap; no scheduled-departures

4 · Take inquiry

Form → CRM → task → respond (auto + human)

5 · Confirm booking

Card on file; deposit + balance; instant confirm; voucher sent

6 · Publish

Trip goes live, IG/SEO/GEO surfaces, day-by-day published

7 · Operate

Day-of logistics, transfers, guides, support

Where the other ICPs branch off the Island Tour operational model

Build site — Advisor: personal brand surface, not a catalog. "I focus on luxury family travel in SE Asia" positioning page. Less product, more portfolio. CMS is portfolio-grade, not catalog-grade.
Add product — Advisor: NO owned products. The advisor "adds" nothing — they assemble from supplier net-rates. The platform surfaces supplier inventory aggregated from 3-5+ external booking systems.
Manage inventory — Boutique/Luxury: "tailormade" = blank-slate itinerary templates, not a fixed catalog. The product IS a per-client proposal; the operator's CMS is for templates + components (hotels, transfers, experiences), not full trips.
Manage inventory — Safari: per-camp inventory. Each camp has a capacity ("6 tents") per date. Cross-camp itinerary = cross-property availability check. The hardest inventory model in the set.
Manage inventory — Multi-Day Tour: scheduled-departure codes (SSEP, SASY, SSKU). A trip is run multiple times per year; each departure has its own seat count. Per-departure pricing overrides (Was/Now sale pricing).
Confirm booking — Quote-then-confirm ICPs: no card on file at inquiry. Operator/agent produces a proposal, client approves, then payment link is sent. State machine has 4–7 steps vs Island Tour's 2-step (book → pay).
Publish — Multi-Day Tour: date-first homepage. "Costa Rica in October" is a higher-traffic landing page than the trip itself. Search + date picker is the primary navigation, not destination picker.
Operate — Safari: inter-camp transfers are first-class inventory. A 7-day trip that moves through 3 camps has 4 transfers; each transfer is its own product with its own capacity and supplier.
Operate — Honeymoon: room-block + guest manifest. The couple is the buyer, but a 30-person wedding party may need 12 rooms. Each room has its own payer, its own check-in, its own dietary preferences.
Operate — Advisor: commission split tracking. Every booking flows commission to multiple suppliers per the host-agency contract. The advisor's role is orchestration + commission collection, not inventory.

TL;DR for the visual

Shared across all 6 ICPs (universal SaaS core): tenancy, auth, CRM, itinerary builder, booking, payment, CMS, website, mobile app, media library. This is what every agency SaaS gets right — and what doesn't differentiate.

Island Tour's anchor position: simplest data model (single-destination, ~10–25 fixed products, instant book, deposit+balance, no ATOL/ABTA). Best v1 ICP because the modes + modules pattern is easiest to prove here. Three modules light up: schools_program, conservation_pages, tailormade.

What gets harder as you move right in the lineup: Travel Advisor (B2B commission model), Boutique/Luxury (quote-then-confirm + trust accounting), Safari (per-camp inventory + transfers as first-class objects), Multi-Day Tour (scheduled-departure codes + trip-style taxonomy), Honeymoon (room block + per-guest ledger). Each one is buildable as a mode + a few modules — but not as a "config switch" on top of Island Tour's model.
