# Tripwheel — Web UI Spec

> **Superseded by [`v0.4.2.md`](v0.4.2.md)** (companion to `prototypes/v0.4.2-states.html`). This file is kept for history.

Owner: Shad · shad@tripwheel.io
Scope: Signup, Dashboard, Social Media Management Tool, Photo Tool, and the coming-soon trip tools.
Style: Buffer-like, neutral. Single accent, generous whitespace, calm SaaS surfaces.

This document specifies the screen-by-screen nested UI hierarchy, component states, and key copy. It is the written companion to `prototypes/v1-base.html`.

## Foundations

The prototype reuses the tokens already in `design-tokens.json`. Brand colors are still `TODO`, so the prototype uses a neutral palette with a single placeholder accent (a calm blue, `#2563eb`) that can be swapped once branding lands.

| Token group | Used for |
|---|---|
| `color.neutral` | Page background (`100`), surfaces (`0`), borders (`200`), secondary text (`500`), primary text (`900`) |
| Accent (`#2563eb` placeholder) | Primary buttons, active nav, links, focus rings, status accents |
| `color.semantic` | Post status pills, validation, toasts |
| `spacing` 4–64px scale | All gaps, padding, gutters |
| `borderRadius` md/lg | Cards, inputs, buttons |
| `shadow` sm/md | Card elevation, popovers, dropdowns |

Type scale and weights come straight from the tokens file (base font `TODO` → prototype falls back to a system stack until set).

## 1. Signup

Goal: account creation with the five fields requested. Split layout — form left, brand/value panel right.

```
SignupPage (centered, no app shell)
├── BrandPanel (right, hidden < 900px)
│   ├── Logo
│   ├── Headline "Run your trips and your socials in one place"
│   └── Feature bullets (Schedule posts · Bulk-edit photos · More tools soon)
└── SignupCard (left)
    ├── Header
    │   ├── Title "Create your account"
    │   └── Subtitle "Start your 14-day free trial. No card required."
    ├── Form
    │   ├── Field · Name            (text, required)
    │   ├── Field · Email           (email, required, inline validation)
    │   ├── Field · Company         (text, required)
    │   ├── Field · Password        (password, show/hide toggle, strength meter)
    │   ├── Field · Confirm password(password, show/hide, match validation)
    │   ├── Checkbox · "I agree to the Terms & Privacy Policy"
    │   └── Button · "Create account" (primary, full width)
    ├── Divider "or"
    ├── SocialAuth (Continue with Google · Continue with Apple)
    └── Footer link "Already have an account? Log in"
```

Field states: default, focus (accent ring), filled, error (red border + helper text), disabled (during submit). Password strength meter: weak/fair/strong using semantic warning→success. Confirm-password shows a check icon when it matches. The submit button is disabled until all required fields are valid and the terms box is checked; on submit it shows a spinner and "Creating account…".

Validation copy: email → "Enter a valid email address"; password → "Use at least 8 characters"; confirm → "Passwords don't match."

## 3. Social Media Management Tool

Buffer-style. Lives under the "Social Media" nav item with its own internal tabs. Below are the distinct states/screens.

### Internal layout

```
SocialMediaTool
├── ToolHeader
│   ├── Title "Social Media"
│   ├── Tabs (Queue · Calendar · Channels · Analytics)
│   └── PrimaryCTA "Create post"
└── TabContent (varies)
```

### 3a. Empty state (no channels connected)

```
EmptyState (centered in content area)
├── Illustration (neutral, line-art social glyphs)
├── Title "Connect a channel to get started"
├── Body "Link your Facebook and Instagram accounts to schedule and publish posts."
├── PrimaryButton "Connect a channel"
└── SecondaryLink "See how it works"
```

### 3b. Connect social handles (modal/flow)

```
ConnectChannelModal
├── Header "Connect a channel"
├── ChannelList
│   ├── ChannelOption · Facebook   (logo, "Connect" button → OAuth)
│   └── ChannelOption · Instagram  (logo, "Connect" button → OAuth)
├── AfterAuthState (per channel)
│   ├── AccountCard (avatar · handle · page/profile name · "Connected" check)
│   └── Toggle "Enabled for publishing"
└── Footer "Done"
```

Connected channels then render as filter chips/avatars at the top of the Queue and as a managed list under the Channels tab (with Reconnect / Disconnect / token-expired warning states).

### 3c. Scheduled posts list (Queue) — the core view

```
QueueTab
├── FilterBar
│   ├── ChannelChips (All · Facebook · Instagram, multi-select)
│   ├── StatusFilter (All · Scheduled · Draft · Published · Failed)
│   └── ViewToggle (List · Calendar)
├── DateGroup (e.g., "Today", "Tomorrow", "Thu, Jun 25")
│   └── PostCard ×N
│       ├── Thumbnail (image/video preview)
│       ├── ChannelBadges (FB / IG icons)
│       ├── CaptionPreview (truncated, 2 lines)
│       ├── ScheduledTime
│       ├── StatusPill (Scheduled · Draft · Published · Failed)
│       └── RowActions (Edit · Duplicate · Delete · ⋯)
└── EmptyWithinFilter ("No posts match these filters")
```

Status pill colors map to semantic tokens: Scheduled = info, Draft = neutral, Published = success, Failed = error (with a "Retry" affordance). Posts are grouped by date like Buffer's queue; an "Add post" ghost row sits at the top of each day group.

### 3d. Create / Edit post (composer)

The composer is one component reused for create and edit (Edit pre-fills it). Two-pane: editor left, live preview right.

```
PostComposer (modal or full screen)
├── Header (title "Create post" / "Edit post" · Close)
├── ChannelSelector (pick FB and/or IG; preview adapts per channel)
├── EditorPane (left)
│   ├── CaptionTextarea
│   │   ├── CharacterCounter (per-channel limit)
│   │   └── Toolbar (emoji · hashtag · mention · link)
│   ├── GenerateCaptionButton  ──► see 3f
│   ├── MediaArea
│   │   ├── DropZone / Upload (images, video)
│   │   ├── ThumbnailStrip (reorder, remove)
│   │   └── EditPhotoButton (per image) ──► see 3e
│   ├── FirstComment (optional, IG/FB)
│   └── SchedulingControls
│       ├── RadioGroup (Add to queue · Schedule for… · Post now · Save draft)
│       └── DateTimePicker (when "Schedule for…")
├── PreviewPane (right)
│   ├── PreviewTabs (Facebook · Instagram)
│   └── PostPreviewCard (account header · media · caption · meta — channel-accurate)
└── Footer (Save draft · Schedule/Queue primary button)
```

Validation: caption-too-long warning, "Add media" reminder for Instagram, per-channel preview switching. Edit mode adds a "Last saved" timestamp and a "Discard changes" affordance.

### 3e. Edit photos (in-composer image editor)

```
PhotoEditorOverlay
├── Header (image name · Done · Cancel)
├── Canvas (image with live transform)
├── ToolRail (left or bottom)
│   ├── Crop (free / 1:1 / 4:5 / 16:9 / story 9:16)
│   ├── Rotate / Flip
│   ├── Adjust (Brightness · Contrast · Saturation · Warmth — sliders)
│   ├── Filters (preset thumbnails)
│   ├── Text overlay
│   └── Watermark toggle (reuse Photo Tool watermark)
└── Footer (Reset · Apply)
```

Applying returns the edited image into the composer's thumbnail strip with an "edited" marker.

### 3f. Generate caption (AI)

```
GenerateCaptionPanel (popover or right drawer from composer)
├── Header "Generate caption"
├── Inputs
│   ├── PromptField "What's this post about?"
│   ├── ToneSelect (Friendly · Professional · Playful · Inspirational)
│   ├── LengthSelect (Short · Medium · Long)
│   └── Toggle "Include hashtags" · Toggle "Include emoji"

│   └── ResultCard (caption text · "Use this" · "Regenerate" · copy)
└── Footer note "AI-generated — review before publishing"
```

"Use this" drops the text into the caption textarea; the character counter and preview update live.

## 4. Photo Tool

Goal: bulk image processing + watermarking with AI-powered automation. Batch-job model: upload many images, apply a pipeline, review, export.

```
PhotoToolPage
├── ToolHeader (Title "Photo Tool" · Tabs: New job · Job history · Watermarks · Presets · CTA "New job")
├── NewJobFlow
│   ├── Step 1 · Upload
│   │   ├── DropZone (drag many / browse / import from a connected source)
│   │   └── UploadGrid (thumbnails · per-file progress · remove · count + total size)
│   ├── Step 2 · Configure pipeline
│   │   ├── ProcessingOptions (panel of toggles)
│   │   │   ├── Resize / Resolution (presets + custom)
│   │   │   ├── Format & Compression (JPG/PNG/WebP · quality slider)
│   │   │   ├── Crop / Aspect (apply to all)
│   │   │   └── Rename pattern (tokens: {trip}-{index})
│   │   ├── AIAutomation (panel — the AI-powered part)
│   │   │   ├── Toggle · Auto-enhance (exposure/color/sharpness)
│   │   │   ├── Toggle · Auto-crop to subject
│   │   │   ├── Toggle · Background cleanup / remove
│   │   │   ├── Toggle · Auto-tag & alt-text generation
│   │   │   └── Toggle · Smart watermark placement (avoids subject)
│   │   └── Watermark (panel)
│   │       ├── WatermarkSelect (saved watermarks) / Upload logo
│   │       ├── Position grid (9 anchors) + Auto (AI)
│   │       ├── Sliders (Opacity · Scale · Margin)
│   │       └── LivePreview (watermark over a sample image)
│   ├── Step 3 · Review
│   │   ├── BeforeAfterGrid (toggle compare · per-image)
│   │   ├── BatchSummary (count · operations applied · est. output size)
│   │   └── PerImageOverride (re-edit a single image)
│   └── Step 4 · Process & Export
│       ├── RunButton "Process {N} images"
│       ├── ProgressBar (X of N · ETA · cancel)
│       └── ResultActions (Download all (zip) · Save to job history · Push to Social composer)
├── JobHistoryTab
│   └── JobRow ×N (name · count · operations · watermark · status · date · Re-run · Download)
└── WatermarksTab
    └── WatermarkCard ×N (preview · name · default toggle · edit/delete)
```

Empty state (Photo Tool, no jobs): centered DropZone with "Drag images here or browse" plus a one-line "AI auto-enhance and watermark in one batch." States to design: uploading, processing (with per-image and batch progress), partial failure (some images failed → retry just those), and complete.

## First-run & empty states

The prototype has a **"View as" switch** in the topbar (New account / Active account) so you can see the difference. Signup lands you in New-account mode.

### Dashboard — right after signup
No populated panels. Instead: a welcome line, all four KPI cards reading `0` with muted "Nothing yet" labels, a **Get started checklist** (0 of 3 — Connect a social account · Schedule your first post · Run your first photo job, each with its own action button), an empty "Connected accounts" panel, and the coming-soon teasers. The checklist is the primary job of this screen; each item deep-links into the relevant flow and checks off as it's completed.

### Social — state 1: no channels connected
Full-area empty state: icon, "Connect a channel to get started", one-line explanation, primary **Connect a channel**, and a "See how it works" link. The Channels tab mirrors this with its own empty state. Nothing else is actionable until a channel exists.

### Social — state 2: connected, but no posts yet
After connecting (Connect modal → pick Facebook/Instagram), the tool advances automatically. The Queue now shows the connected accounts as "Publishing to" chips at the top, then an empty-queue state: "Your queue is empty / Your accounts are connected. Schedule your first post and it'll show up here, grouped by day." Primary action **Create your first post** plus a secondary "Import from a CSV". The Channels tab now lists the connected accounts.

### Social — state 3: active
The populated Buffer-style queue grouped by day, with scheduled/draft/failed posts.

### Photo Tool — no jobs yet
The New job tab shows a single centered drop zone — "Process your first batch / Drop in your trip photos and Tripwheel will auto-enhance, crop, and watermark the whole batch" — instead of the multi-step configure flow. Job history and Watermarks tabs would show their own empty states.

The transition is wired in the prototype: connecting a channel moves Social from state 1 → state 2 and fires a toast.

## Cross-cutting states & patterns

Loading uses skeleton rows/cards (not spinners) for lists; spinners only for button actions. Errors use an inline alert at the top of the affected panel plus a toast for transient failures. Empty states always pair a one-line explanation with a single primary action. All destructive actions (delete post, delete watermark, disconnect channel) confirm via a small dialog. Responsive: sidebar collapses to icons below 1024px and to a top hamburger below 768px; the composer's two panes stack on mobile with preview behind a tab.
