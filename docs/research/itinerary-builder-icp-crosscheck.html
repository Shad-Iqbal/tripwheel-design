Itinerary Builder × ICP Cross-Check

Does the v0.5.5 itinerary builder serve the six agency ICPs' jobs and painpoints — and where does it stop short of the research's architecture?

v1.0 · 1 Jul 2026 · Compiled for Shad / Tripwheel

Sources: prototypes/v0.5.5-states.html (itinerary builder) × docs/research/island-tour-vs-agency-icps-combined.html (ICP research v1.1)

Section 1

Executive Summary

The finding in one paragraph. The v0.5.5 itinerary builder is a day-by-day experiential trip planner and client-facing proposal tool — not a booking, pricing, or inventory engine. In the research's architecture it sits inside the shared ~60% core (it is the "Itinerary Builder" every ICP needs) and it implements the Itinerary entity's draft and shared states. But its shape quietly commits to one workflow: a human-crafted, shareable proposal — the research's quote mode. That makes it an excellent fit for Luxury and a strong-but-incomplete fit for Advisor, while leaving the recommended v1 anchor Island Tour's actual money-path (fixed catalogue → instant book → deposit) entirely outside the tool.
Headline tension. The research names Island Tour as the v1 anchor because it is the simplest data model (fixed_catalogue + instant book). The builder, however, is architected like a bespoke quote-mode proposal tool. So the tool as-built is most complete for the ICPs the research recommends deferring, and least complete for the anchor's conversion flow. The decision this report surfaces: does the builder grow a pricing + quote→confirm status ladder, or does that live in a separate Bookings/Payments module?
Section 2

What the Builder Is

Read from the prototype source, the tool does the following and only the following:

  • Brief capture — trip name, destination, client, start date, length, adults/kids + note, budget tier ($/$$/$$$), pace (relaxed / balanced / packed), and interest chips (Food, Culture, Nature, Nightlife, Shopping, Relax, Adventure, History).
  • Three start modes — Blank, Template, or AI draft (AI tailors day suggestions to the brief's pace and interests).
  • Day builder — reorderable stops, each with a category (Sight / Food / Nature / Transit), duration in minutes, a note, and photos; a Leaflet map with auto-estimated intra-city transit (walk / metro / train); editable day title + date; and a trip "overview" (intro note, must-dos, don'ts).
  • Templates — a reusable library tagged by category (Beach & Island, City Break, Multi-city, Honeymoon, Adventure) with "used N×" counts.
  • Sharing — a live, no-login link; WhatsApp / email / copy; PDF download or send; a branded client viewer ("Planned by Alex Rivera · Northwind Travel Co."); and a client → advisor reply loop.
  • Statuses — only two: Draft and Shared.
What it is not. There is no price or cost anywhere (budget is a $ tag with no totals), no booking step, no payment or deposit/balance, no inventory or availability, and no status beyond Draft/Shared. Its transit model is city micro-transit (walk/metro/train), not inter-city or charter transfers.

Section 6

The Anchor Tension, in Detail

The research is explicit: Island Tour is the cleanest v1 anchor because it is fixed_catalogue, mostly instant-book, mostly deposit + balance — the simplest data and content model. The traveler journey for that ICP is browse → discover → compare 2–3 tour variants → instant book with deposit.

The builder, by contrast, implements the quote-mode journey: capture a brief, assemble a bespoke day-by-day by hand (or from AI/template), then share a live proposal and collect a reply. That is the Advisor / Luxury pattern — the research's own "4+ step, no card-on-file at inquiry" flow — not the anchor's two-step book→pay.

The consequence is a mismatch of completeness. The builder is closest to done for the two ICPs the research recommends deferring or licensing (Advisor is called "a fundamentally different data model… recommend deferring"), and furthest from done for the anchor it recommends shipping first. This is not a defect in the builder — it is a strong proposal tool — but it means the builder alone does not make the Island Tour v1 sellable. The catalogue + instant-book + deposit surface must exist alongside it.

Section 7

Gap Analysis: What to Add, What to Defer

Grouping the missing pieces by how central they are to shipping, and how hard the research says they are to retrofit.

Add to the builder / adjacent (blocks a sellable v1)

  • Pricing: per-stop / per-day / trip totals — unlocks a real quote for every ICP.
  • Status ladder: extend Draft/Shared → Quote-sent → Accepted → Confirmed → Paid.
  • Instant book + deposit/balance: the anchor's conversion path (may live in a Bookings module the builder hands off to).
  • Lead / CRM link: a lead pipeline behind the client attachment.

Defer to per-ICP modules (research: hardest to retrofit, but post-MVP)

  • Honeymoon: room_block + per-guest ledger + COMPARE.
  • Safari: per_date_inventory, holds, inter-camp transfers, trade_portal.
  • Multi-Day: scheduled_departure picker, codes, seats, waitlist, physical rating.
  • Advisor: commission_split, supplier net-rates.

Universal gaps that matter across all six

Three absences show up for every ICP, not just one: price/cost anywhere, any booking or payment step, and any status beyond Draft/Shared. These are the difference between a presentation tool and a proposal tool. Closing them is what turns the builder from a beautiful share-link into the commercial spine of the quote-mode ICPs.

Section 8

Recommendations

  • Keep the builder as the shared-core proposal surface — don't overload it. It is already an excellent day-by-day planner and client viewer. Inventory, payments, and per-ICP modules should not be crammed into it; they belong to modes/modules the builder hands off to.
  • Add the pricing + status ladder next — it is the single highest-leverage gap. Per-component pricing and a Quote-sent → Accepted → Confirmed → Paid status chain unlock Advisor, Luxury, Honeymoon, and Safari quote flows at once, with one shared change rather than four bespoke ones.
  • Decide the Island Tour money-path deliberately. The anchor needs a fixed-catalogue browse + instant-book + deposit surface that the builder does not provide. Either build that as a sibling Bookings module for v1, or accept that v1's first sellable ICP is a quote-mode agency (Luxury), not Island Tour — and re-anchor accordingly.
  • Wire templates to the tailormade module concept explicitly. Templates are the one module the builder already gestures at. Naming and structuring them as tailormade starting points keeps the door open for Luxury / Safari / Island Tour reuse.
  • Treat the reply loop as the seed of CRM. The client → advisor reply is a lightweight thread today. Behind it should sit the lead/CRM pipeline every ICP's JTBD assumes (Advisor JTBD 6, Luxury JTBD 1).
Bottom line. The builder is a correct and polished piece of the shared ~60% core, and the best-in-class part of the quote-mode experience. To make it load-bearing for a sellable v1, add pricing and a quote→confirm status ladder, and make an explicit call on whether the Island Tour anchor's instant-book path is built now (as a sibling module) or deferred in favour of launching a quote-mode ICP first.
Section 9

Method & Sources

This cross-check was produced by reading the prototype source and the ICP research end-to-end, then mapping observed builder capabilities against the research's feature matrix (Section 8), jobs-to-be-done (Section 6), and modes/modules architecture (Section 9). "Fit" ratings are qualitative judgements from the JTBD alignment, not a measured score. No feature was inferred from UI labels alone — each was confirmed in the prototype's data model and handler logic.
