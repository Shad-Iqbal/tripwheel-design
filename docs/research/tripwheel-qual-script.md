# Tripwheel — Qual Interview Script
> **Format:** 20-min video call
> **Structure:** 15 min problem interview (Mom Test) → 5 min vague reveal + reaction
> **Interviewers:** Shad + Yousuf (one asks, one takes notes — never both talking)
> **Hypotheses:** H4 (AI discovery awareness), H5 (price willingness)
> **Rule:** Never pitch. Never lead. Ask about the past, not the hypothetical.

---

## Pre-call Setup

**Assign roles before every session:**
- **Interviewer:** Asks questions. Follows the script loosely. Does not take notes.
- **Note-taker:** Records exact words used, not summaries. Timestamps key moments. Does not speak unless asked.

**Have open:** Survey response for this person. Note their Q6 (top frustration) and Q10 (open text) before the call — reference these to open naturally.

**Record:** Ask permission at start. "Mind if we record this? It's just for internal notes — nothing shared."

---

## Opening (1 min)

"Thanks for making time. This is genuinely a research conversation — we're not going to pitch you anything. We want to understand how you work, what frustrates you, what works well. Your perspective helps us figure out if what we're building is worth building.

I'll ask most of the questions. [Note-taker name] is here taking notes so I can focus on listening.

Sound good? Let's start."

> **Note-taker:** Start recording now.

---

## Part 1 — Their World (5 min)

**Goal:** Understand current situation before going anywhere near pain. Let them talk. Don't rush to the problems.

---

**Q1 — Open the floor**

"Before anything else — tell me a bit about your travel business. What kind of trips do you specialise in, and how long have you been doing it?"

> Listen for: size, specialisation, how they describe themselves. This tells you the beachhead fit. Advisors who say "I do everything" are weak ICP. Advisors who say "I specialise in luxury Maldives honeymoons" are strong ICP.

---

**Q2 — Current digital setup**

"Walk me through your current website situation — what you're using, when you set it up, what you like and don't like about it."

> Do NOT mention platforms. Let them name their own tools. If they say "WordPress" or "Squarespace," just nod. If they say "I don't really have one," follow up: "How do clients find you then?"

> Listen for: how long ago setup, who built it, who maintains it, tone (pride vs. frustration vs. resignation).

---

**Q3 — Client acquisition**

"How do most of your clients come to you today?"

> Listen for: referral-heavy vs. digital. This is context for H4. If they say "Google" or "Instagram," the AI question lands harder. If they say "all referrals," you'll need to build the case for why digital matters before asking about AI.

---

## Part 2 — Pain Excavation (8 min)

**Goal:** Find the real pain with specific stories and costs. Mom Test rule: "What's the last time X happened?" beats "does X happen to you?" every time.

---

**Q4 — Find the real problem**

"What's the most frustrating part of managing your online presence right now?"

> Wait for the answer. Do not fill silence. If they say something vague like "it's just a lot of work," follow up:

**Follow-up:** "Can you give me a specific example? Like, last time you had to update something — what was that like?"

> You want a story, not a category. Stories have costs attached. "I spent four hours last Tuesday trying to change the photos on my homepage" = real pain. "It's time-consuming" = category, not pain.

---

**Q5 — Proposal / presentation workflow (H7 probe)**

"How do you typically present a trip to a client after they've reached out? Walk me through what actually happens."

> Listen for: tools used, time taken, how many back-and-forths, what the client experience is like. This is H7 depth.

**Follow-up if they mention PDFs or Canva:**
"How long does it take you to put one of those together?"

**Follow-up if they seem satisfied:**
"Has a client ever given you feedback on that — positive or negative?"

---

**Q6 — Cost of the problem**

"Have you ever lost a client — or think you might have — because of how you showed up online? Or because of how a proposal came across?"

> This is the most important question. A "yes" with a specific story = validated pain with real cost. A "no" = either no pain, or they haven't connected the dots. Don't push if they say no.

**If yes:** "Tell me about that."

**If no:** "What would have to change for you to feel like your online presence was genuinely working for you?"

---

**Q7 — Tools and time (H2 + H3 depth)**

"What tools are you currently paying for to run your business? Anything from website hosting to CRM to proposal tools."

> Let them list. Count tools in your head. If 3+, confirm: "And are these all talking to each other, or do you have to copy things between them?"

**Follow-up:**
"Roughly how much are you spending across all of those per month — if you had to guess?"

> This is financial context for H5. Don't lead them to a number. Their unprompted estimate is the anchor.

---

**Q8 — AI awareness (H4)**

"Last question before I share what we're working on — have you ever received an inquiry from someone who said they found you through ChatGPT or Perplexity or another AI tool?"

> Most will say no. That's expected.

**If no:**
"Are you doing anything specifically to show up in AI search results — or is that something you've thought about?"

> Listen for: awareness level (never heard of it / aware but not acting / actively trying). This is H4. Don't explain what GEO is unless they ask — that would lead them.

**If yes:** "Tell me about that." Then pause the script. This is a gold moment — document everything.

---

## Part 3 — Reveal + Reaction (4 min)

**Goal:** Gauge interest in the product direction without pitching specific features. Get honest "yes I need this" or "meh" reactions.

---

**Transition**

"Thanks — this has been really helpful. Can I share what we're working on and get your reaction?"

> Wait for yes. Don't assume.

---

**The reveal (keep it to 3–4 sentences max)**

"We're building a platform that gives travel professionals a much stronger digital presence — specifically built for how travel is actually sold. The idea is that everything — your website, how you present trips, how clients find you — works together as one system instead of five different tools. And it's built in a way that makes you visible to AI tools the way a good website makes you visible on Google.

That's the short version. Honest reaction — does that solve something real for you, or does it feel like a nice-to-have?"

> Listen for the first instinct. Pause after asking. Do not explain further. You want the gut reaction before they self-censor into politeness.

---

**Q9 — Price willingness (H5)**

*Only ask this if they showed genuine pain earlier. Don't ask a satisfied respondent — you'll get garbage data.*

"If something like this existed and worked exactly as you'd want — what would you expect to pay for it per month?"

> Do not give a range. Do not say "like $50 to $100." Let them anchor first. Whatever they say, follow up:

"What would make you feel like that was worth it — what would have to be true?"

> This is the value anchor. More useful than the price number itself.

---

**Q10 — Close**

"This has been genuinely useful. Last thing — is there anyone else you'd think of who'd be worth talking to? Other travel advisors or agency owners who have strong opinions about this stuff?"

> Referral pipeline question. Warm intros grow faster than cold outreach inside travel communities.

---

## Closing (1 min)

"That's everything. I really appreciate your time — you've given us a lot to think about.

We'll keep you posted as we get closer to having something to show. Would it be alright to come back to you with a few questions as we get further along?"

> Set expectation: you'll follow up. This is not a one-time conversation — these people become your founding member pipeline.

## Signal Scoring (post-call)

Score each session immediately after. Don't wait — memory fades.

| Signal | Strong (3) | Moderate (2) | Weak (1) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Pain depth | Specific story + cost | Vague frustration | "It's fine actually" |
| Reveal reaction | "When can I use this?" | "Interesting idea" | "I don't really need that" |
| Price willingness | Named a number without prompting | Said "depends" | Wouldn't engage |
| Referrals given | 2+ names | 1 name | None |

**Session score 10–12:** Strong signal. Fast-track to founding member outreach.
**Session score 6–9:** Moderate. Keep warm, re-engage at launch.
**Session score below 6:** Weak. Note the segment + reason. Don't chase.

---

## Rules During Every Session

- Ask about the **past**, not the hypothetical. "Have you ever..." not "Would you ever..."
- When they finish answering, wait 3 seconds before speaking. Silence makes people add more.
- If they ask "what does the platform do specifically?" — say "We're still figuring that out based on conversations like this. What would you want it to do?"
- Never say "most people we talk to say..." — you're not selling, you're learning
- If they start pitching you their idea for what to build — listen and note it, don't commit to anything
- End on time. 20 minutes. Respect that they agreed to 20 minutes.
