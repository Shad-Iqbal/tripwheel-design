## Recommendations

**Use Option 1** as the primary. Strongest for cold traffic that arrives without context. Problem-recognition copy stops the scroll.

**Use Option 3** for survey link shared in communities. People clicking from a Facebook group already have context — don't over-explain.

**Option 2** for LinkedIn posts — curiosity framing works better in a professional feed than problem-first.

---

## Copy Notes

**Tone rules:**
- Talk to one person, not "travel professionals" in aggregate. "You" not "agencies."
- No buzzwords: no "seamless," "all-in-one," "game-changer," "revolutionary."
- No exaggeration about what we'll build. We don't know yet — and the copy shouldn't pretend we do.
- Short sentences. Travel agency owners are busy. Every sentence earns its place.

**Things NOT to say on the landing page:**
- Anything that sounds like a feature list
- Pricing
- What the platform specifically does (reveals product)
- Comparisons to competitors by name
- "We're building the Shopify for travel" — overused and pitchy

**CTA button copy options (test these):**
- "Share your experience" — lowest friction, humble
- "Take the 4-minute survey" — sets time expectation
- "Help shape what we build" — gives them ownership
- "Tell us what's broken" — bold, works for frustrated users
